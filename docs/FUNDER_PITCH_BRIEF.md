# CareAround SG Funder Pitch Brief

Source: `docs/CAREAROUND_SG_PROJECT_DOSSIER.md`, generated from the current repository working tree.

## 30-Second Pitch

CareAround SG helps seniors, caregivers, and community partners find and organise senior-related support in Singapore. It is already more than an idea: the current product includes discovery, resource detail pages, saved resources, personal maps, shared maps, partner/admin resource management, imports, translations, and privacy-aware access controls. The next step is a focused pilot that proves CareAround SG can reduce service-navigation friction while keeping users, partners, and data safe.

## 2-Minute Pitch

Finding senior-related support is often not a single search. Families need to compare nearby places, check programme details, understand eligibility, save useful options, and share those options with someone else. Partners and AAC staff also need a practical way to keep listings current without turning every update into a manual spreadsheet exercise.

CareAround SG addresses this by combining a public discovery experience with a partner/admin maintenance layer. Users can browse places and programmes, save resources, build My Maps, print or export them, and share view-only map links. Partners and administrators can manage resources, service areas, audience zones, memberships, imports, translations, and partner-only notes/files.

The product is a working pre-pilot platform, not a concept deck. The responsible next step is a small pilot with clear scope, privacy safeguards, partner review, and aggregate impact metrics. AI should be introduced carefully: rule-based matching first, human review, clear explanations, audit logs, consent, and only then AI assistance for summaries, data quality, and support-matching explanations.

## Problem

Seniors, caregivers, and families often struggle to move from "support exists" to "these are the realistic options we can compare, save, and share." Resource information may be fragmented, hard to understand, location-dependent, eligibility-dependent, outdated, or difficult to turn into a practical planning artifact.

Partners also face a maintenance problem: resource listings, programme details, translation review, private operational notes, and imports need structured workflows rather than scattered manual updates.

## Solution

CareAround SG provides:

- Public discovery of senior-related places and programmes.
- Resource detail pages with context users can check before acting.
- Saved resources and My Directory.
- My Maps for planning, printing, image export, and sharing.
- Shared map links for family or helper collaboration.
- Partner/admin resource management.
- Workbook import/export and Google place import.
- Translation and multilingual review foundation.
- Partner-only notes/files.
- AI-assisted data preparation as a review-first staff tool.

## Why Now

- Singapore's ageing population increases the need for practical service navigation.
- Caregivers and family members need tools that turn information into action.
- Community partners need better ways to maintain accurate resource information.
- AI can help with data preparation and future matching, but only if governance is built first.
- A small pilot can test impact, trust, and workflows before larger investment.

## Why CareAround SG

- It is already being built, with working product surfaces in the repository.
- It combines public user journeys with partner/admin maintenance workflows.
- It is map-led, shareable, printable, and practical for real caregiver planning.
- It has foundations for multilingual support and accessibility.
- It recognises that AI should be human-reviewed, explainable, and non-clinical.
- It is designed to keep public users free while creating legitimate partner/funder value.

Working or foundational areas include:

- Discover, resource details, saved resources, My Directory, My Maps, and shared maps.
- Authentication, Google sign-in, phone-login/phone-identity foundations.

- Workbook import/export.

## Impact Metrics

Senior/caregiver outcomes:

- Users who save at least one resource.
- Maps created, printed, exported, or shared.
- Self-reported ease of finding relevant support.
- Time saved preparing a shortlist.

Partner outcomes:

- Partner staff onboarded.
- Listings reviewed or updated.
- Data freshness improvement.
- Translation review completion.
- Staff satisfaction with maintenance and sharing workflows.

Service-navigation outcomes:

- Search-to-detail conversion.
- Detail-to-save conversion.
- Saved-resource-to-map conversion.
- Shared-map views or copies.

Governance outcomes:

- No sensitive-data leakage through public snapshots, shared maps, or exports.
- AI suggestions remain human-reviewed.
- No AIC endorsement or official-agency claim is made.

## Sustainability Model

Keep free:

- Public discovery.
- Saving resources.
- My Directory and My Maps for standard users.
- Basic sharing/printing for seniors and caregivers.

Possible partner/funder value:

- Partner dashboard access and onboarding.
- Resource-maintenance workflows.
- Translation review support.
- Aggregate reporting and pilot evaluation.
- Sponsored public-access pilots.
- Data-quality improvement projects.
- Human-reviewed AI assistant features for staff after governance is ready.

Do not monetise too early:

- Public user access.
- Sensitive resource ranking.
- Individual user data.
- Partner-private content.
- AI recommendations before governance maturity.

## AI Roadmap

Near term:

- Keep AI as review-first data preparation for imports and draft resource content.
- Improve rule-based matching using location, category, tags, eligibility, member status, and visibility rules.
- Add transparent "why this appears" explanations.

Pilot term:

- Test human-reviewed shortlists.
- Add consent and audit foundations.
- Use aggregate non-AI reporting first.

Future:

- AI-assisted support-matching explanations after rule-based matching is proven.
- AI-assisted aggregate insight summaries for partners/funders with human approval.
- AI alerts only after notification preferences, consent, opt-out, and safe templates are implemented.
