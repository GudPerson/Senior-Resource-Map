## Slide Notes

### Slide 1: CareAround SG

Suggested headline: "CareAround SG helps seniors and caregivers find, save, and share community support options."

Speaker notes: CareAround SG is an independent product under development. It is built for Singapore community-care navigation and should be reviewed with clear COI and endorsement safeguards.

Avoid: "AIC platform", "official directory", "approved ecosystem product".

### Slide 2: The Navigation Gap

Suggested headline: "The problem is not only finding information. It is turning information into practical options."

Speaker notes: Families often need to compare location, eligibility, schedule, availability, and confidence before acting. Partners need a way to keep information current.

Avoid: unsupported statistics, clinical outcome claims, or statements that imply all current resources are verified.

### Slide 3: Who It Serves

Suggested headline: "Designed around public users and trusted maintainers."

Speaker notes: The product serves guests, standard users, caregivers, partner staff, admins, and funders. Each group has different needs and permissions.

Avoid: saying all user groups are active users unless confirmed.

### Slide 4: What Users Can Do Today

Suggested headline: "The core public journey already exists."

Speaker notes: Users can browse Discover, open detail pages, save resources, build My Maps, and share map links.

Avoid: "fully launched", "national scale", or "production-proven" unless separately validated.

### Slide 5: From Search To Planning

Suggested headline: "My Maps turns useful resources into a shareable planning artifact."

Speaker notes: This is especially helpful for caregivers, family discussions, and helper coordination.

Avoid: "care plan", "referral", or "case management record".

### Slide 6: Partner/Admin Layer

Suggested headline: "Partners can maintain the data that users depend on."

Speaker notes: The product includes resource management, imports, translations, visibility rules, and private operational notes/files.

Avoid: claiming partner workflows are final without pilot confirmation.

### Slide 7: Trust And Safety By Design

Suggested headline: "Public and private content are separated."

Speaker notes: The app includes role-based access, security headers, rate limits, validation, private content separation, and public/legal pages. Audit and consent depth should be strengthened before scale.

Avoid: "fully compliant", "privacy-certified", or "risk-free".

### Slide 8: AI: Careful, Human-Reviewed

Suggested headline: "AI helps prepare data; people remain accountable."

Speaker notes: The repository supports AI-assisted import/data improvement and translation workflows. AI service matching, analytics, and alerts remain planned/partial.

Avoid: "AI prescribes", "AI recommends services automatically", "AI replaces staff review".

### Slide 9: Pilot Concept

Suggested headline: "Start small: one service area, one partner cluster, 8-12 weeks."

Speaker notes: A narrow pilot protects users, partners, and product quality while generating useful evidence.

Avoid: naming partners, sites, or endorsement unless confirmed.

### Slide 10: What We Will Measure

Suggested headline: "Measure navigation value and data quality first."

Speaker notes: Focus on saves, maps, shares, listing freshness, translation readiness, partner updates, and feedback.

Avoid: clinical improvement claims, cost-saving claims, or attribution beyond the pilot evidence.

### Slide 11: Funding Logic

Suggested headline: "Public access stays free; funding supports the infrastructure around it."

Speaker notes: Funder/partner value should come from onboarding, data quality, translation, governance, aggregate reporting, and pilot evaluation.

Avoid: user fees for essential access, data monetisation, or paid ranking.

### Slide 12: The Ask

Suggested headline: "Help CareAround SG move from working product to governed pilot."

Speaker notes: Ask for pilot support, governance review, pilot partner alignment, and funding for evaluation and safeguards.

Avoid: "ready to scale nationally" before pilot evidence.
