# CareAround SG COI Risk Management Plan

Source: `docs/CAREAROUND_SG_PROJECT_DOSSIER.md`, refreshed from the active repository state on 2026-06-08.

Purpose: manage actual, potential, and perceived conflict-of-interest risks before CareAround SG is shared with AIC, AACs, partners, funders, sponsors, or advisors. This is a working plan, not legal advice.

## Actual, Potential, And Perceived COI Risks

| Risk category | Risk | Current status | Management need |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Actual COI | The product owner may have a role, duty, access, decision right, or reporting relationship connected to AIC, AACs, partners, funders, or procurement. | `Needs input` | Confirm with HR/compliance before partner or funder approach. |
| Potential COI | CareAround SG may later seek pilots, funding, data access, or partnerships with organisations connected to the product owner's work. | Likely future risk | Create approval and recusal rules before outreach. |
| Perceived COI | Stakeholders may assume AIC support or inside access because the product is Singapore community-care oriented. | Meaningful risk | Use independent wording and explicit non-endorsement disclaimers. |
| Information-use COI | Non-public AIC, employer, partner, or agency information could be perceived as influencing product design or pitch materials. | `Needs input` | Use repository-grounded, public, demo-safe, or authorised information only. |
| Procurement/funding COI | A pilot, grant, or sponsorship may overlap with procurement or funding decisions connected to the product owner's role. | `Needs input` | Recuse from decisions and seek written guidance. |
| Partner influence COI | A partner or sponsor may expect favourable ranking, visibility, access, or product direction. | Future risk | Adopt sponsor/visibility policy and disclose funding relationships. |
| AI/recommendation COI | Future matching could favour better-funded or better-connected resources over user need. | Planned-feature risk | Keep matching explainable, rule-based first, human-reviewed, and fair. |
| Governance-scope confusion | Organisation access or Admin Region Scope could be misunderstood as resource ownership, private-content access, or public Discover relevance. | Current communication risk | Use explicit wording: Organisation access is governance-only; Admin Region Scope is support coverage only. |
| Security-readiness perception | The parked after-demo security fixes could be mistaken as complete if pitch materials do not separate them from current product maturity. | Current communication risk | Track the 5 fixes separately and avoid pilot-readiness claims until remediation decisions are made. |

## Specific Safeguards

- Disclose CareAround SG to the appropriate HR/compliance contact before any AIC-facing, partner-facing, or funder-facing outreach.
- Keep a written record of all guidance received.
- Maintain independent branding, domain, materials, and communication channels.
- Do not use AIC logos, confidential materials, private contacts, internal templates, or non-public data without written permission.
- Do not imply AIC endorsement, approval, sponsorship, procurement, or operational involvement.
- Use only public, demo-safe, repository-grounded, or expressly authorised data in screenshots, demos, decks, and pilot materials.
- Keep seniors, caregivers, guests, and standard users free for core public access.
- Do not sell individual user data or care-planning context.
- Do not monetise resource ranking or placement unless a formal sponsor/visibility policy is reviewed and disclosed.
- Keep AI support review-first and non-clinical.
- Add consent, audit logs, and reporting thresholds before analytics, alerts, or AI-assisted matching pilots.
- State clearly that Organisation access does not grant resource editing by itself.
- State clearly that Admin Region Scope does not grant resource ownership, private notes/files access, or public Discover relevance.
- Keep after-demo security remediation separate from funder, pilot, and disclosure claims until each fix is completed and verified.

## Recusal Approach

Recusal should apply when the product owner has any role in a decision that could affect CareAround SG or a related partner/funder.

Recommended recusal triggers:

- AIC, employer, partner, or funder discussions involving CareAround SG.
- Procurement, grant, sponsorship, pilot, evaluation, or vendor decisions where CareAround SG could benefit.
- Access to non-public data or partner information that could influence CareAround SG.
- Review of competitors, vendors, or partners in the same product area.
- Any decision where a reasonable observer could perceive divided loyalty.

Recommended recusal steps:

1. Notify the appropriate manager, HR, or compliance owner.
2. Record the potential conflict and the requested guidance.
3. Step out of the decision or discussion until guidance is given.
4. Do not access non-public materials for CareAround SG purposes.
5. Keep a brief record of the recusal decision and scope.

## Partner Approach Rules

- Approach partners only after disclosure guidance is obtained where required.
- Do not use personal access, internal directories, confidential lists, or privileged introductions without permission.
- Use transparent wording: CareAround SG is independent and not AIC-endorsed.
- Ask partners to confirm what data can be used, displayed, imported, screenshotted, or shared.
- Avoid naming a partner in pitch materials until written permission is received.
- Do not present partner interest as partnership, endorsement, funding, or approval until confirmed.
- Keep pilot scope small and documented.
- Require staff review for imported, AI-assisted, or partner-submitted content before public use.
- Document whether Organisation governance, Admin Region Scope, Support Coverage, Audit Trail, and the parked security fixes are inside or outside the pilot scope.

## Funding/Procurement Restrictions

CareAround SG should not:

- Participate in any procurement, grant, or funding process where the product owner has decision influence without prior disclosure and recusal guidance.
- Use AIC or employer information to gain an unfair advantage.
- Present informal feedback as procurement approval.
- Accept funding conditions that require undisclosed preferential resource ranking.
- Accept sponsorship that undermines public trust or user safety.
- Charge seniors/caregivers for essential public discovery and planning access during early pilot stages.

Funding conversations should:

- Be documented.
- Include a non-endorsement statement where relevant.
- Separate public-access funding from resource visibility decisions.
- Identify any sponsor, partner, or funder relationship that could affect perception.
- Preserve user data minimisation and privacy requirements.
- State whether any parked security remediation is expected before pilot launch or only after demo.

## AIC Endorsement Disclaimer

Use this disclaimer unless AIC/HR/compliance provides a different approved wording:

> CareAround SG is independently developed and is not an AIC product. No AIC endorsement, sponsorship, approval, procurement, recommendation, or operational involvement is claimed unless formal written approval is provided by AIC.

Short deck footer:

> Independent product under development. No AIC endorsement claimed.

## Sponsor/Visibility Policy

Principles:

- User relevance, safety, and trust come before sponsor visibility.
- Public resource ranking should not be sold as paid placement in early stages.
- Sponsored pilots may fund access, data quality, translation, onboarding, evaluation, or governance work, but should not override fair discovery.
- Any sponsor relationship that affects content, scope, or visibility must be disclosed.
- Sponsored resources should be labelled if sponsorship creates a reasonable perception of influence.
- AI-assisted matching must not favour sponsors unless the rule is explicit, reviewed, and user-safe.
- Organisation governance, Admin support coverage, and Audit Trail workflows must not be used to imply sponsor control over public resource ranking.

Allowed sponsorship examples:

- Funding free public access.
- Funding a pilot in a defined service area.
- Funding translation, accessibility, or data-quality work.
- Funding aggregate evaluation and reporting.

Restricted sponsorship examples:

- Paid ranking in sensitive community-care search results.
- Sponsor access to individual user data.
- Sponsor influence over AI matching without disclosure and review.
- Sponsor-funded content that appears as official advice.

## Operating Checklist

- Disclosure prepared and reviewed.
- AIC/HR/compliance questions sent or queued.
- Recusal rules documented.
- Partner approach rules documented.
- Non-endorsement wording added to decks and proposals.
- Demo data and screenshots reviewed for sensitivity.
- Sponsor/visibility policy included in funder discussions.
- AI described as review-first and planned carefully.
- Pilot scope avoids clinical advice, official endorsement, and unnecessary personal data.
- Pilot materials do not imply Organisation access grants resource editing or Admin Region Scope grants ownership/relevance.
- Parked after-demo security fixes are tracked separately and not described as complete until remediated and verified.
